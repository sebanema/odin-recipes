Chickpea Curry

Description

Dive into the world of aromatic spices and vibrant flavors with our delightful Chickpea Curry. A heart-warming dish brimming with nutrition and taste, it is a beautiful blend of tender chickpeas, bathed in a sauce rich with earthy spices, tomatoes, and coconut milk. This hearty curry is not just about comfort and taste, but it is also loaded with protein and fiber, making it a perfect balance of healthy and delicious.

The curry is infused with a fragrant medley of spices such as turmeric, cumin, coriander, and garam masala, creating a dish that truly encapsulates the essence of authentic Indian cuisine. The richness of the sauce is beautifully contrasted with the freshness of cilantro and a squeeze of lemon juice added just before serving. Served over a bed of fluffy basmati rice or with a side of warm naan bread, this Chickpea Curry is a true feast to the senses. Each bite is a symphony of flavors, making you come back for more.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium serrano pepper, cut into thirds
  • 4 large cloves garlic
  • 1 2-inch piece fresh ginger, peeled and coarsely chopped
  • 1 medium yellow onion, chopped (1-inch)
  • 6 tablespoons canola oil or grapeseed oil
  • 2 teaspoons ground coriander
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 2 ¼ cups no-salt-added canned diced tomatoes with their juice (from a 28-ounce can)
  • ¾ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 2 15-ounce cans chickpeas, rinsed
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Steps:

  1. Pulse serrano, garlic and ginger in a food processor until minced. Scrape down the sides and pulse again. Add onion; pulse until finely chopped, but not watery.
  2. Heat oil in a large saucepan over medium-high heat. Add the onion mixture and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened, 3 to 5 minutes. Add coriander, cumin and turmeric and cook, stirring, for 2 minutes.
  3. Pulse tomatoes in the food processor until finely chopped. Add to the pan along with salt. Reduce heat to maintain a simmer and cook, stirring occasionally, for 4 minutes.
  4. Add chickpeas and garam masala, reduce heat to a gentle simmer, cover and cook, stirring occasionally, for 5 minutes more. Serve topped with cilantro, if desired.
